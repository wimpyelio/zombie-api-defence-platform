Risk Index — Live FormulacomputeRI() · realtime
RI = (S × E) + (V / A)
S = Sensitivity [0–1] — PII/PCI payload classification (NLP scanner). Independent of exposure.
E = Exposure [0–1] — network topology: 1.0 internet-facing, 0.6 DMZ, 0.2 internal-only.
V = Vulnerability composite — weighted sum of missing controls (see table below).
A = Age in months since last code commit or deployment update.
Note: S and E are orthogonal signals. A highly sensitive endpoint (S→1) with internal-only exposure (E=0.2) is structurally less exposed than a low-sensitivity public endpoint (S=0.1, E=1.0). The formula captures this — S×E is a joint risk product, not a substitution.

⚠ Zombie + PCI auto-response threshold
A zombie (a≥12) with maximum V=1.75 and E=1.0, S=0.9 yields RI≤1.04 — structurally Medium. The PCI auto-response fires at state=Zombie + pciInScope=true + RI>0.8. The Critical band (RI>2.5) flags newly-deployed insecure APIs where A is small: ep16 (a=1, all vulns) → RI≈2.65.
